Actions

Prioritised next steps

Findings consolidate into a ranked plan: behavioural changes, configuration improvements and hardware upgrades.

Behaviour

  • Shift heavy evening loads earlier while solar or battery cover is available
  • Review habit patterns that drive peak grid import

Configuration

  • Adjust battery reserve and discharge timing for evening peaks
  • Tighten export and self-use settings before buying more capacity

Hardware

  • Consider storage or PV only after clipping and scheduling are reviewed
  • Share the PDF with your installer when an upgrade case is clear

FAQ

Answers about reports and credits

Sign-in, credits, tariffs, and what a report covers.

What is inside a Sunsynk Performance Report?

A shareable PDF from about 30 days of plant readings. It moves from an executive overview into data coverage, optional estimated savings, overall health, energy flows, solar and battery analysis, evening grid dependency, accuracy checks, and prioritised recommendations.

Is there a subscription?

No. One credit equals one report. Buy Starter (1), Classic (3), or Pro (5) when you need them. Credits never expire. Unused credits from a purchase can be refunded within 14 days via the Credits page.

Can I trust the data?

Before generation, the app checks about 30 days of telemetry coverage and asks you to confirm if coverage is thin. Each report also includes data coverage, plant details, and a system accuracy check. Assumptions and limitations sit in Important Information in the PDF.

Who is the report for?

Sunsynk account holders. Sign in with your Sunsynk email and password. Installers may also use the PDF when supporting a homeowner aftercare conversation.

How does report generation work?

Sign in at app.sunsynkreports.com, buy credits if your balance is zero, optionally save a tariff under Settings for estimated savings, then generate a report for a plant. You can leave the page while it prepares; when ready, preview and download the PDF. One credit is used per report.
